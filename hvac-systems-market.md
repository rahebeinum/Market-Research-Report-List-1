<p><p>HVAC systems are categorized into decentralized and centralized markets based on their configuration and control. Decentralized HVAC systems operate independently within individual spaces or zones, allowing for tailored temperature control and energy efficiency in diverse areas, commonly used in smaller buildings. In contrast, centralized HVAC systems utilize a central unit to manage the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning for an entire building. This approach often enhances energy efficiency and ease of maintenance, making it suitable for larger facilities.</p></p>
