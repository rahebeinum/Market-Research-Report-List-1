<p><p>The Vacuum Insulating Glass (VIG) market is divided into two main types: tempered VIG and non-tempered VIG. Tempered VIG undergoes a heat treatment process, enhancing its strength and resistance, making it suitable for high-stress applications like facades and high-rise buildings. In contrast, non-tempered VIG lacks this additional treatment, offering less structural integrity but maintaining excellent thermal insulation properties. Both types cater to diverse architectural needs, focusing on energy efficiency and reducing heat transfer in buildings.</p></p>
