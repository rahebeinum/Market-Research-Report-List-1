<p><p>Ethylene Dichloride (EDC) is a chemical primarily used in the production of vinyl chloride. The market for EDC can be segmented by production processes, including direct chlorination and oxychlorination. Direct chlorination involves the reaction of ethylene with chlorine at elevated temperatures, producing EDC with minimal by-products. In contrast, oxychlorination uses ethylene, chlorine, and oxygen to produce EDC, typically yielding higher purity and efficiency. Both methods influence market dynamics based on production costs and environmental considerations.</p></p>
